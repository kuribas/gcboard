Features

GCBoard has the following features, including those most often found in chess interfaces such as XBoard:

  • An easy to use API

  • Automatic support for:

    • dragging
    • animated moves
    • flashing moves
    • highlighting last moves
    • premove (not finished)

    All these may be these may be turned off by the user.

  • Custom placement of pieces in the square. Automatic positioning in one of the cardinal directions or centering.
  • A very powerful overlay system: You may add custom pixmaps to the board in a layered fashion. THey can be placed under the pieces or over them.
  • Cross platform: adding new platforms and toolkits is very easy (see documentation).
  • Fast animation (double buffered). This makes the library useful to be used with languages that have a slow interpreter.
  • Support for more then one grid (i.e. a rectangular area to place the pieces), for example in Shogi to place the captured pieces.
  • Drawing the background and highlighting can be handled either by gcboard or by your application.
  • Support for the Gtk2 toolkit.
  • Support for Ruby using ruby-gtk2
